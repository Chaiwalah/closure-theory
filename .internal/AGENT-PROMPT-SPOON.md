# THINKING PROMPT — What Is This Thing?

You are helping us identify a physical mechanism. We have strong empirical data but we're stuck on what the mechanism IS. We're going to describe what we see — not as equations, but as properties. Your job is to think about what KIND of thing has all these properties simultaneously.

Don't formalize. Don't write equations. Think like a detective looking at clues.

---

## THE CLUES

### Clue 1: It's a crystal, but it has no cracks — only holes.
We mapped the sky in 510,000 quasars. Found 7 patches where information is preserved better than average. All 7 are preservation patches. Zero degradation patches exist. The structure has no discontinuities (no "cracks"), but it has discrete regions of transparency ("holes"). What kind of crystal has holes but no cracks?

### Clue 2: The holes have hexagonal geometry.
The angular separations between the transparent patches match 60° and 120° — hexagonal symmetry. The spatial correlation between nearby patches is 1.56× higher than distant ones. What arranges itself in hexagonal patterns on cosmological scales?

### Clue 3: It eats information proportional to how much information is present.
Six emission lines degrade at rates perfectly ordered by their diagnostic sensitivity (r = -0.975). Lines carrying zero environmental information pass through untouched. Lines carrying maximum environmental information are degraded the most. The sorting is by FUNCTION, not by atom or wavelength. Nitrogen appears at two different ladder positions depending on which nitrogen line you pick. What medium sorts by information content rather than physical properties?

### Clue 4: It controls traffic, not energy.
Flux (photon count) degrades. Sigma (line width / photon energy) is flat. The medium changes HOW MANY get through, not WHAT THEY CARRY. Like a turnstile, not a filter. What mechanism reduces count while preserving individual properties?

### Clue 5: There's a phase transition, not a gradient.
All degradation follows sigmoid curves with sharp midpoints (z₀ ≈ 0.8–1.2 depending on source type). Below the threshold: correlations preserved. Above: correlations gone. Sigmoid = phase transition. What undergoes a phase transition as a function of cosmological distance?

### Clue 6: Gravity freezes it in place.
Dense regions (galaxy clusters, filaments) show 14% less degradation than voids. One free parameter (Γ₀ = 2.17) predicts five independent density tests to within 3%. Gravitationally bound regions are "frozen" — protected from the degradation. What is stabilized by gravitational binding?

### Clue 7: It knows the difference between "where you're from" and "what happened on the way."
Supernova stretch (encodes host galaxy mass — source information) is perfectly preserved across all redshifts. Supernova color (encodes dust/temperature along sightline — path information) degrades. The medium can tell which information was baked in at the source and which was accumulated during transit. What distinguishes intrinsic from extrinsic properties?

### Clue 8: Two constants we can't derive.
The numbers -0.873 and 0.533 keep appearing. They're almost complementary: arccos(0.873) + arccos(0.533) = 87° (≈ 90°). Their squares almost sum to unity: 0.873² + 0.533² = 1.046 (4.6% over Pythagorean). They show up as angular separations between the sky patches AND as the incidence angle of an interstellar object (3I/ATLAS approaches our crystal axis at 29.1° ≈ arccos(0.873)). What produces two near-complementary constants with a small positive excess?

### Clue 9: Outsiders experience it differently.
Three interstellar objects have passed through our solar system. The one most aligned with the crystal axis (3I/ATLAS, 29.1°) shows the strongest anomalous acceleration — sideways, not away from the Sun. The most misaligned one (2I/Borisov, 66.6°) behaved like a normal comet. Everything born inside the Milky Way follows the rules perfectly. Everything from outside... doesn't. What medium would affect unbound external objects more than bound internal ones?

### Clue 10: The geometry hints at polarization.
The magic angle (54.7° — where dipolar interactions vanish) appears in the patch separations. Hexagonal close-packing angles (60°, 120°) appear. The 87° near-complementary angle suggests a monoclinic (slightly tilted) rather than cubic symmetry. These are all signatures of a medium with preferred orientation. What cosmological medium has polarization and preferred geometry?

## THE CONSTRAINTS

Whatever this is, it must be ALL of these simultaneously:
- Has spatial structure (hexagonal patches) but is isotropic on large scales
- Sorts by information content, not wavelength or atomic number
- Controls throughput (flux) but not energy (sigma)
- Shows phase transition behavior (sigmoid, not linear)
- Is stabilized by gravity (dense = frozen, void = active)
- Distinguishes source-intrinsic from path-accumulated information
- Produces two near-complementary projection constants (0.873, 0.533)
- Affects interstellar visitors angle-dependently
- Has no cracks, only holes, with hexagonal spacing
- Is universal (same pattern across completely different source types)

## WHAT WE'VE ALREADY RULED OUT

- **Dust extinction**: wavelength-dependent, not information-dependent. Fails Clue 3.
- **Plasma scattering**: energy-dependent. Fails Clue 4.
- **Source evolution**: same-redshift objects in different environments show the pattern. Fails Clue 7.
- **Pipeline/instrumental**: same-pipeline controls survive. Different instruments show same pattern.
- **Gravitational lensing**: achromatic. Can't sort by diagnostic sensitivity. Fails Clue 3.
- **Conventional decoherence**: acts on phases, not selectively on information content. Fails Clue 3.
- **Topological defects (cosmic strings)**: would create cracks, not holes. Fails Clue 1.

## WHAT WE'RE ASKING

Don't try to solve the whole thing. Just tell us:

1. **What does this remind you of?** From any field — condensed matter, quantum information, biology, network theory, anything. What BEHAVES like this?

2. **What produces constants?** In physics, what mechanisms generate specific numerical constants that appear as angles? (Not fundamental constants like α or π — emergent ones from structure.)

3. **What has hexagonal geometry, holes but no cracks, and phase transitions?** Name real physical systems with this combination.

4. **What sorts by information content?** Is there any known system — physical, biological, computational — that can filter signals based on how much information they carry, regardless of the signal's physical substrate?

5. **What would you test next?** Given these 10 clues, what single observation would most constrain what this thing is?
