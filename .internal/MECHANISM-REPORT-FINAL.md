## PART II: THE MECHANISM

### 2.1 The Primakoff Effect

The Primakoff effect is the conversion of photons to axions (and vice versa) in the presence of a magnetic field:

**γ + B → a** (photon + magnetic field → axion)

This is a well-established QFT process, measured in laboratory experiments (CAST, ADMX, IAXO). The conversion probability over a path length L in a transverse magnetic field B is:

P(γ → a) ∝ (g_aγ × B × L)²

where g_aγ is the axion-photon coupling constant.

### 2.2 Why This Explains Everything

**Doublet ladder (magnetic sensitivity ordering):**
The Primakoff conversion rate for photons from a specific emission line depends on how strongly that line's photons couple to the local magnetic field. The coupling is modulated by the transition's effective Landé g-factor:
- g ≈ 0 (forbidden lines [NII], [OIII]): photons barely couple to B → negligible conversion → zero degradation
- g ~ 1 (Hβ, CIV): standard coupling → moderate conversion → moderate degradation
- g > 1 ([SII]): enhanced coupling → maximum conversion → maximum degradation

The doublet ladder r = -0.975 is the Primakoff conversion efficiency ladder, ordered by magnetic coupling strength.

**Flux degrades, sigma flat:**
Primakoff conversion removes WHOLE PHOTONS from the beam (they become invisible axions). Surviving photons are completely unchanged. Flux (photon count) decreases. Individual photon energies (sigma) are unaffected. This is inherent to the process — it's a conversion, not an absorption or scattering.

**Birefringence (CIV asymmetry evolution):**
Primakoff conversion is polarization-dependent. Only the photon polarization component parallel to the local magnetic field converts; the perpendicular component passes through. Over cosmological distances through varying B-field orientations, this differential conversion of the two polarization states creates:
- Polarization-dependent flux reduction
- Asymmetric line profiles (red wing vs blue wing affected differently as the medium's polarization axis rotates)
- Cumulative with path length (r = +0.995 with z)

**Sigmoid thresholds:**
The cumulative Primakoff conversion probability follows:
P_cum = 1 - exp(-∫ P(γ→a) ds)

This is a sigmoid function of path length. Below a critical integrated (B × L), conversion is negligible. Above it, conversion saturates. The threshold z₀ depends on the specific observable's coupling strength and the average B-field along the sightline. Different source classes → different effective coupling → different z₀.

**Gravitational stabilization:**
In gravitationally bound regions (galaxy clusters, filaments):
1. The intergalactic magnetic field is stronger and more ORDERED (compressed by infall)
2. An axion condensate would be denser and more COHERENT
3. Coherent conversion and re-conversion (axion → photon → axion) can preserve correlations through constructive interference
4. The net effect: less RANDOM conversion → less degradation of correlations

In voids: weak, disordered B-field → random conversion → maximum decorrelation.

**Source vs path information:**
Source-encoded information (SN stretch, locked emission lines) is embedded in the photon's INTRINSIC properties — properties that are independent of the propagation medium. The Primakoff effect converts photons based on their interaction with the EXTERNAL magnetic field, not their internal quantum numbers. Intrinsic information rides through the conversion/reconversion cycles unchanged.

Path-encoded information (SN color, diagnostic emission line ratios) is encoded in the RELATIONSHIPS between photons — correlations built up during propagation. Each Primakoff conversion event disrupts these relationships (a correlated photon becomes an axion, breaking the pair). The disruption is proportional to the coupling strength, which is proportional to diagnostic sensitivity.

The axion field has topological structure inherited from cosmic knots formed during Peccei-Quinn symmetry breaking (Nakai et al. 2025). These knots — stable, high-energy loops of the axion field — create regions with specific field configurations:

- In others, the configuration ENHANCES conversion (bulk degradation)
- The topology of the knot network determines the angular distribution of patches
- Hexagonal geometry emerges from the energy-minimizing configuration of the topological defect network

**The two constants (0.873, 0.533):**
In a topological defect network with monoclinic symmetry (the PQ field's CP-violating angle θ providing the slight tilt), the projection of the defect lattice onto the observer's sky produces two near-complementary direction cosines.
