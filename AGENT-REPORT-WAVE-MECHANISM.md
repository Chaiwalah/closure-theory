## The Question We Asked

We stepped back from percolation math and asked something basic:

**Light is both a particle and a wave. The particle (energy) is conserved — we measure redshift, flux, arrival time, all consistent. But the wave carries something the particle doesn't: correlational structure — the relationships between observables (EW, FWHM, luminosity, line ratios).**

Our data across 130 tests shows:
- Energy arrives faithfully (particle-honest)
- Correlational structure degrades at z ≈ 1 (wave-dishonest)

So: **what if the anomalies we've been documenting — the sigmoid collapses, the lattice reorganization, the doublet ladder — aren't "information loss" but rather the signature of the electromagnetic wave equilibrating with the medium along its path?**

A photon isn't a rigid postcard. It's an oscillating E-field and B-field. As it propagates through magnetized plasma, expanding space, density gradients — the wave adapts. The E-field and B-field components participate unevenly depending on local conditions. Faraday rotation (proven physics, λ²-dependent) is one known example. But nobody has checked whether cumulative wave-medium equilibration systematically alters the correlational structure of observables at cosmological scales.

## The Clue

We ran sightline environment tests — splitting 750K quasars by sky direction at fixed redshift. If z were the only variable, all directions should show the same correlational structure.

They don't.

**Galactic longitude l ≈ 292° (the Shapley Concentration direction) shows anomalous correlational degradation at z = 0.8–1.0:**

| Pair | Shapley direction (Q4) | Rest of sky | Δ |
|------|----------------------|-------------|---|
| MgII↔Hβ (optical, 4861Å) | +0.595 | +0.697 | **−0.101** |
| MgII↔CIII (UV, 1909Å) | +0.729 | +0.731 | −0.002 |

The anomaly is:
1. **Sightline-specific** — only in the direction of the most massive structure in the observable universe
2. **Wavelength-dependent** — affects the optical correlation (longer λ) but NOT the UV correlation (shorter λ)
3. **Consistent with Faraday rotation** — which goes as λ², so optical > UV

The Shapley Concentration (~10¹⁶ solar masses) has enormous magnetic fields in its intracluster medium. The wave passes through it. The optical component (longer wavelength) gets affected more. The UV component survives.

This is one clue. Small. But it's in the right direction, at the right wavelength, affecting the right pair.

## The Implication

If electromagnetic wave-medium interaction along the path systematically alters correlational structure:
- The sigmoid at z ≈ 1 isn't a "phase transition in vacuum" — it's cumulative wave equilibration crossing a detection threshold
- The N_modes hierarchy (CIV breaks first, MgII-CIII survives) could reflect wavelength/complexity-dependent susceptibility to path effects
- The lattice reorganization (optical → UV backbone) could reflect optical wavelengths accumulating more path damage than UV
- The Faraday rotation mechanism (proven, λ²-dependent, path-integrated) is already in the textbook — nobody applied it to correlational structure
