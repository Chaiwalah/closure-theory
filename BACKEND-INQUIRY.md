# Backend Inquiry — Multi-Model Creative Prompt

## The Prompt (send to each model separately, no context about Closure Theory)

---

**PROMPT:**

I'm a systems engineer thinking about an abstract problem. No physics background needed — I want engineering intuition.

Imagine a system that processes signals. The signals are generated by sources, travel through a medium, and arrive at a detector. Think of it like a massive distributed network.

Here's what I've observed empirically:

1. **Signals generated long ago arrive with less internal structure than recent ones.** Not weaker — same total energy — but the correlations between signal components are degraded.

2. **The degradation is selective.** Some components (call them "locked" — they have fixed ratios built into their generation mechanism) arrive perfectly intact. Other components (call them "diagnostic" — their ratios depend on local environmental conditions at the source) are degraded proportionally to how much environmental information they carry.

3. **The path doesn't matter.** Two signals that travel nearly identical paths through the medium but were generated at different times show different degradation. Two signals generated at the same time but traveling very different paths show identical degradation.

4. **The medium appears uniform.** No preferred direction, no hot spots, no cold spots. The degradation is isotropic.

5. **Information isn't destroyed — it migrates.** The correlation between components doesn't monotonically decay. It oscillates. The information moves between channels rather than disappearing.

6. **The source's internal dynamics gate the effect.** High-velocity, high-activity sources are more affected. Massive, quiet sources are shielded. But the effect still requires time (older signals) — source properties alone don't cause it.

7. **Broadband signals are immune.** Signals that are blends of thousands of overlapping frequencies pass through unaffected. Narrow, well-defined frequency components are vulnerable.

8. **The total throughput is conserved.** Detection rate × correlation strength is roughly constant across all time periods.

**My question:** What kind of backend mechanism could produce ALL of these properties simultaneously? 

I'm not asking you to match this to known physics. I'm asking you to engineer a system that behaves this way. What would the medium have to be? What would the mechanism of interaction look like? Think about it from information theory, signal processing, network engineering, condensed matter — whatever framework lets you reason about it.

Give me your best 2-3 candidate mechanisms. For each one, explain:
- How it produces selective degradation (locked vs diagnostic)
- How it's path-independent but time-dependent
- How it conserves information while redistributing it
- What would break it (what observation would kill this explanation)

Don't hedge. Pick the mechanisms you think are most likely and commit to them.
