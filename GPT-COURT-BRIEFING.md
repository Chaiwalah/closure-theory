## The Prosecution's Case

### Exhibit A: Bell's Theorem (Replicated, Nobel Prize 2022)

**What it proves:** A measured local property of a quantum system does NOT represent a pre-existing global state. The measurement outcome is contextual — it depends on the measurement configuration.

- Aspect (1982): Violated Bell inequalities experimentally
- Zeilinger et al. (2015): Loophole-free Bell test
- Nobel Prize 2022: Awarded specifically for proving local realism is false

**The principle:** You CANNOT measure a property locally and assume it was that value before measurement, or that it holds the same value globally. This is not interpretation — this is replicated experimental fact.

**The inconsistency:** Cosmology measures G, c, ℏ, α locally and assumes these values held globally across all spacetime, including epochs we cannot access. This is exactly the extrapolation Bell's theorem forbids at the quantum level. If a single electron's spin cannot be assumed from measurement, on what basis do we assume the fine-structure constant held at t = 10⁻⁴³s?

### Exhibit B: The Singularity Admission

Physics openly states:
- General relativity breaks down at the singularity
- Quantum mechanics cannot describe the Planck epoch
- "Physics as we know it" does not apply before t = 10⁻⁴³s

**The inconsistency:** We admit our laws break at the origin, then use those same laws to describe what the origin produced. We cannot describe the event, but we claim to know its output.

Analogy: "I can't see inside the factory or describe how it works, but I know exactly what it manufactured." That's not science. That's faith wearing a lab coat.

### Exhibit C: Black Hole Singularity (Known Physics)

A single stellar-mass black hole (~10 M☉) creates a singularity where:
- GR predicts infinite curvature
- Time dilation becomes infinite
- Light cannot escape
- Known physics breaks

The Big Bang singularity was the ENTIRE observable universe compressed. If physics breaks at stellar-mass compression, it absolutely breaks at cosmological compression. Yet we assume continuity of laws through the more extreme event.

### Exhibit D: The Circular Derivation

1. We observe the universe TODAY
2. We derive laws from CURRENT observations (G, c, expansion rate)
3. We run those laws backwards
4. We arrive at "the Big Bang"
5. We say "the Big Bang produced the universe that gave us these laws"

This is circular. The output (current universe) is used to define the input (Big Bang), which is then said to explain the output. At no point is the initial state independently constrained.
