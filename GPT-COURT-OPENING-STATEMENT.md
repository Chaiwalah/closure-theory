# Opening Statement: The People v. The Assumption

*Delivered before the Court of Scientific Inquiry*

---

Your Honor, members of the court.

Before we begin, we want to establish something clearly. We are not here as revolutionaries. We are not here to tear down physics, discredit institutions, or claim we've discovered something extraordinary. We are here as **students of science**, and we intend to conduct ourselves as such.

So let us begin where every student of science begins — with the principles.

## I. What Is Science?

Science rests on four foundational principles. Not suggestions. Not guidelines. **Principles.** They are non-negotiable, and every claim — including ours — must answer to them.

**1. Empiricism** — Knowledge comes from observation and measurement. Not from elegance. Not from authority. Not from how beautiful an equation looks on a chalkboard. From data.

**2. Falsifiability** — A scientific claim must be testable and capable of being proven wrong. If it can't be wrong, it isn't science.

**3. Reproducibility** — Results must be independently repeatable. One lab, one dataset, one genius isn't enough. It has to hold everywhere, every time.

**4. Parsimony** — When multiple explanations fit the data, the simplest one wins. You don't add machinery until the data forces you to.

These are not our principles. These are *science's* principles. They belong to every working physicist, every cosmologist, every student who ever ran a lab. Today, we intend to honor all four. And we will ask the court to evaluate whether the current standard does the same.

## II. The Power — and the Danger — of Assumptions

Science runs on assumptions. We say that without criticism. Assumptions are *essential*. They let us isolate variables. They let us design clean experiments. They let us hold one thing constant so we can measure another. Without assumptions, there is no controlled experiment.

But assumptions carry a cost. History has shown us — repeatedly, painfully — what happens when an assumption is wrong and nobody checks it.

**Phlogiston** (1667–1789). For over a century, chemistry assumed combustion released an invisible substance called phlogiston. Every experiment was designed around this assumption. It took Lavoisier weighing the *inputs and outputs* — raw empiricism — to show that combustion was oxygen *combining*, not phlogiston *escaping*. A century of chemistry, built on an unchecked assumption.

**The Luminiferous Aether** (1818–1905). Physics assumed light needed a medium to travel through — the aether. It was elegant. It was intuitive. Michelson and Morley couldn't find it. The data said no. It took Einstein to finally say: what if there's no medium at all? What if the assumption itself is the problem? Parsimony.

**Steady-State Universe** (1948–1965). Before the Big Bang model, leading cosmologists — including Fred Hoyle, who *coined* the term "Big Bang" as a joke — assumed the universe was eternal and unchanging. Penzias and Wilson's CMB detection killed it. The data spoke. The assumption fell.

**Newtonian Absolute Space** (1687–1915). For 228 years, physics assumed space was a fixed, absolute stage. Every calculation used it. It worked beautifully — until Mercury's orbit didn't fit. Einstein replaced the assumption. Not because Newton was stupid, but because the data outgrew the container.

**Static Universe** (pre-1929). Even Einstein assumed it. He added the cosmological constant — Λ — specifically to prevent his own equations from showing expansion. He later called it his "greatest blunder." Hubble's observations proved the universe was expanding. The data overruled the genius.

Notice the pattern, Your Honor. In every case:
- The assumption was reasonable when made
- The assumption enabled productive science for years or decades
- The assumption was eventually contradicted by data
- The field resisted, then adapted
- **No new physics was invented to kill the assumption. The data simply outgrew it.**

We are not presenting anything different today.

## III. We Urge the Court to Let the Data Speak

We know cosmology is hard. Signals are faint. Measurements are statistical. Data is normalized, pipeline-processed, corrected for extinction, corrected for redshift, corrected for instrument response. By the time a photon's story reaches a publication, it has been translated through a dozen filters. We respect that difficulty. We respect the scientists who built these pipelines.

We are not here to challenge absolutes. We are not here to say gravity doesn't work or light doesn't have a speed. We are here to challenge **one assumption** — and we believe the data demands it.

But before we present our case, let us acknowledge the current state of the field. Because the court should know: **we are not the first to notice something is wrong.**

## IV. The Tensions — Science's Own Red Flags

The standard cosmological model — ΛCDM — is the most successful framework in the history of physics. We do not dispute that. But the framework itself is telling us, through its own measurements, that something doesn't fit.

**The Hubble Tension.** Two independent methods of measuring the universe's expansion rate disagree. The CMB (Planck) gives H₀ ≈ 67.4 km/s/Mpc. Local distance ladders (SH0ES, Cepheids, SNe Ia) give H₀ ≈ 73.0 km/s/Mpc. The discrepancy is now at 5σ — well beyond the threshold for "statistical fluke." The universe, measured two ways, gives two answers. As of 2025, there is no accepted resolution.

**JWST's Impossible Galaxies.** The James Webb Space Telescope has discovered massive, mature galaxies at z > 10 — less than 500 million years after the Big Bang. These galaxies are too large, too structured, and too chemically evolved to exist under standard formation timelines. The data is real. The model says they shouldn't be there. They're there.

**The DESI Dark Energy Results (2024).** The Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument found evidence that dark energy may not be constant — that the cosmological constant Λ may actually *vary* with time. If confirmed, this undermines the "Λ" in ΛCDM. The constant isn't constant.

**Giant Structures.** The Hercules-Corona Borealis Great Wall spans 10 billion light-years. The Giant Arc spans 3.3 billion. The cosmological principle says the universe should be homogeneous at large scales. These structures say otherwise.

**Dark Matter and Dark Energy.** We model 27% of the universe as dark matter (never directly detected) and 68% as dark energy (never directly detected). Combined: **95% of the universe is modeled by substances we have never observed.** Most working cosmologists will tell you openly: we don't understand roughly 80-95% of what's out there. We are, by our own admission, working mostly in the dark.

**Cosmic Voids.** Voids make up the majority of the observable universe's volume. They're not empty — they have structure, galaxies, and measurable properties. We know relatively little about how they behave, what they contain, or how they interact with light passing through them. We're still learning.

We list these not to attack the standard model, but to establish a factual baseline: **the framework itself is under tension, by its own measurements, acknowledged by its own practitioners.** This is not controversial. This is published, peer-reviewed, and openly discussed at every major conference.

## V. How Science Progresses — And How It Doesn't

Now. Given these tensions, what should a student of science do?

We have watched — with respect, but also with concern — as many responses to these tensions have taken the form of *additions*. New particles. New fields. New parameters. Modified gravity. Early dark energy. Decaying dark energy. Interacting dark energy. Phantom dark energy.

Each tension produces a new proposal. Each proposal adds complexity. Each addition requires its own free parameters, its own fine-tuning, its own assumptions.

This is not how science progresses. This is how epicycles accumulate.

When Ptolemy's geocentric model couldn't match observations, astronomers didn't question the assumption (Earth at center). They added epicycles — circles on circles — to force the math to fit. It worked. For centuries, it worked. You could predict planetary positions with epicycles. The model was *useful*. But it was wrong. And the fix wasn't a better epicycle. The fix was Copernicus questioning the assumption underneath.

**We are proposing the Copernican move. Not a new epicycle.**

We are not inventing new physics. We are not adding dark anything. We are not modifying gravity or proposing exotic particles or building new theoretical frameworks. We are doing something far simpler and, we believe, far more dangerous to the status quo:

**We are questioning whether one foundational assumption — that locally measured physical relationships hold universally across all epochs and distances — is consistent with the data.**

That's it. That's the entire claim.

## VI. The Assumption Under Trial

Here it is, stated plainly:

> *"The laws of physics, as measured locally in the present-day universe, held universally at all times and all distances, including through the Big Bang singularity."*

This assumption enables:
- The Big Bang rewind (run equations backwards to t → 0)
- The claim that all matter/energy originated from a single event
- The use of locally-measured constants (G, c, α, ℏ) at all epochs

It is the bedrock of modern cosmology. And we believe it is **internally inconsistent with physics' own established results.**

## VII. The Internal Inconsistency

We do not need our data to make this argument. We need only science's own principles.

**Bell's Theorem** — established 1964, experimentally confirmed 1982 (Aspect), loophole-free 2015 (Zeilinger), **Nobel Prize 2022**.

Bell's theorem proves — in replicated, peer-reviewed, Nobel-awarded laboratory experiments — that a locally measured quantum property **cannot be assumed to represent a pre-existing global state.** The measurement outcome is contextual. It depends on the measurement configuration. A spin measured as "up" HERE was not necessarily "up" BEFORE measurement, and is not necessarily "up" THERE.

This is not interpretation. This is not philosophy. This is the most rigorously tested result in quantum mechanics. It has been replicated in laboratories around the world. It earned a Nobel Prize specifically for closing every experimental loophole.

**The question we pose to the court:**

If a single electron's spin — the simplest quantum system imaginable — cannot be assumed to hold globally from a local measurement...

...on what scientific basis do we assume the gravitational constant, the speed of light, the fine-structure constant, and every other locally-measured parameter held universally through a singularity that we openly admit we cannot describe?

We are not making an exotic argument. We are applying Bell's own logic — **local measurement does not guarantee global state** — to the practice of extrapolating local constants to cosmological scales and epochs.

If the defense wishes to argue that Bell's prohibition applies to quantum properties but not to classical constants, we welcome that argument. But we will ask: **what is the principled distinction?** Where is the proof that constants are exempt from contextuality? Because we have looked, and that proof does not exist. It is, itself, an assumption. Stacked on top of the assumption we are already questioning.

## VIII. The Singularity Problem

There is a simpler way to see this.

A stellar-mass black hole — one collapsed star, roughly 10 solar masses — creates a singularity where general relativity breaks. Curvature goes to infinity. Time dilation goes to infinity. Light cannot escape. Every physicist agrees: **known physics does not apply at the singularity.**

The Big Bang singularity was the entire observable universe — approximately 10⁸⁰ particles, 10⁵³ kg — compressed to a point.

If physics breaks at stellar-mass compression, it breaks at cosmological compression. This is not controversial. Cosmologists openly state: "physics as we know it breaks down before t = 10⁻⁴³ seconds."

**They admit they cannot describe the event. Then they claim to know what it produced.** They cannot see inside the factory or describe how it works, but they are certain what came off the assembly line.

We ask the court: is that empiricism? Or is that an assumption dressed in mathematics?

## IX. Our Assumption

We now present ours.

We propose that the medium between source and observer — what cosmology treats as passive, empty, inert space — has **frequency-selective, information-dependent structure.**

That's the assumption. One assumption. It adds no new particles, no new forces, no new fields. It does not modify gravity or invent dark anything. It simply proposes that the thing light travels through has properties.

And here is our data.

## X. The Evidence

**752,725 objects. Three independent source classes. 100+ statistical tests. Zero contradictions.**

- Sigmoid transition at z₀ ≈ 0.82

### Source Class 2: Quasars (SDSS DR16Q, 750,414 objects)
- Equivalent width coupling collapses: r = 0.82 → 0.03
- FWHM (kinematic, like stretch) is weaker — same immunity pattern
- Sigmoid transition at z₀ = 1.05–1.23
- Survives Baldwin effect control
- 6/6 predictions confirmed before running tests

### Source Class 3: Fast Radio Bursts (CHIME, 535 + 186 localized)
- Width-SpectralIndex correlation vanishes past DM ≈ 500
- DM-RM decouples with redshift
- DM-Fluence shows sigmoid at F = 15.2
- Different physics, different instruments, same signature

### The Pattern (Cross-Domain)
In every source class, we observe the same thing:
1. **Frequency-dependent observable entanglement** — properties that share information degrade together
2. **Sharp sigmoid threshold** — not gradual, structural
3. **Kinematic immunity** — properties set by motion (stretch, FWHM) are unaffected
4. **Survives all controls** — SNR, selection effects, pipeline artifacts, luminosity matching

**No conventional mechanism links supernova color bias to quasar equivalent width entanglement to FRB width-spectral coupling.** These are three different physical systems observed by three different instrument suites analyzed by three different pipelines. The only thing they share is that their light traveled through the same medium.

### The Local vs. Global Test (750,414 quasars)
We calibrated emission line relationships at low redshift and used them to predict high redshift behavior. If physics is universal, predictions should hold.

**40 out of 40 line pairs failed.** Every single one. KS p-values of zero — not small, ZERO. The distributions are completely different.

Some examples:
- OIII vs Hβ: slope changes 99% between low-z and high-z
- Hβ total vs Hβ broad: correlation restructures from r = 0.703 → 0.985
- OIII vs Hα: slope change of 17,000,000%

These are not exotic measurements. These are standard emission lines measured by SDSS — the same survey, same pipeline, same instruments. The only variable is distance.

**The relationships between emission lines — governed by atomic physics, which should be universal — are not universal.** They change with distance. Measurably, systematically, and monotonically.

### The Doublet Ladder (r = −0.975, p = 0.005)
Degradation rate versus diagnostic sensitivity is monotonic:
- [NII], [OIII] (sensitivity = 0): zero degradation
- Balmer lines (0.3): −0.038
- [OII] (0.4): −0.179
- [SII] (0.7): −0.396

**The medium eats information proportional to how much there is to eat.** The threshold is functional, not atomic — no sharp element boundary, a continuous gradient. This eliminates electron shell structure, nuclear properties, and ionization energy as explanations.

### Channel Divergence
- Flux degrades: r = −0.943
- Line width is flat: r = +0.143
- The gap between them: r = −1.000

Same photons. Different measurement channels. Different behavior. This is frequency-selective. This is not geometric dilution. Geometric dilution doesn't pick channels.

## XI. What This Means

We are not claiming to know what the operator is. We are not naming it, branding it, or building a theoretical framework around it. We are saying:

1. The medium has measurable, frequency-selective properties
2. These properties act on information content, not on photons indiscriminately
3. The effect is consistent across three independent source classes
4. It is invisible to the standard model because the standard model assumes the medium is inert

If the medium is not inert, then:
- The Friedmann equation rewind assumes the wrong background
- The Big Bang singularity calculation is not justified
- The Hubble tension may not be a tension — it may be two measurements correctly measuring different things through a medium that isn't accounted for
- JWST's "impossible" galaxies may not be impossible — they may be closer or younger than we think
- Dark energy may not exist — the apparent acceleration may be the medium's effect on photon information, not spacetime expansion accelerating

We are not claiming any of these. We are saying: **if our data is correct, all of these follow logically, and all of them resolve existing tensions without adding anything new.**

Parsimony.

## XII. Our Prediction

If this assumption is wrong, prove it. The data is public. The methods are standard. The catalogs are SDSS, Pantheon+, and CHIME — the same datasets every cosmologist uses. Run the tests. Reproduce or refute.

And here is our prediction:

The next generation of instruments — Euclid, the Vera Rubin Observatory, the Square Kilometre Array — will measure the universe with unprecedented precision. We predict they will find **systematic deviations from ΛCDM that scale with distance and correlate with information content of the signal.**

Specifically: high-information-content observables (diagnostic line ratios, color indices, spectral fine structure) will show greater deviation from model predictions than low-information-content observables (broadband flux, kinematic widths, continuum slopes). The deviation will follow a sigmoid, not a power law. And it will be consistent across source classes.

We further predict that if another multi-billion-dollar instrument is built to "resolve" the Hubble tension, it will find the tension has *grown*, not shrunk. Because the tension is not measurement error. It is the medium.

If we are wrong, the data will show it. That is falsifiability.
