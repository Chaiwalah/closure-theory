## Executive Summary

Five tests were run against public cosmological data to look for signatures of information-channel degradation ("Closure drift") in Type Ia supernova observations.

**One test produced a strong signal. The others were null.**

- ✅ **Test 4** (accumulation): Color–distance coupling strengthens monotonically with redshift. r goes from -0.17 → -0.71. All bins significant. This is consistent with path-length accumulation.
- ❌ **Tests 1-3** (baryonic cross-match): No correlation between SN observables and Planck tSZ y-map at any angular scale.
- ⚠️ **Test 5** (model comparison): Marginal — the accumulation interaction term (y×z) shows a slight improvement but doesn't beat BIC penalty.

## Test 2: Survey-Split Replication

14 surveys tested independently. No consistent signal across surveys. One marginal hit (FOUNDATION μ_resid: r=-0.17, p=0.03) and one small-N anomaly (CfA3S χ²/dof: r=0.43, p=0.04, N=24) — both consistent with multiple comparisons noise.

**Verdict: NULL.** No replicating signal.

## Interpretation

### What the data says:
1. **Color–distance coupling accumulates with redshift** — this is real, statistically robust, and present in every z-bin.
2. **This accumulation does NOT correlate with Planck tSZ y** — the baryonic column density along the line of sight, as measured by Planck, has zero relationship with SN observables.

### What this means for Closure Theory:
The Test 4 result is **consistent with** but **not unique to** Closure Theory. The accumulation pattern could also be explained by:
- **Selection effects**: at high-z you only detect certain types of SNe (Malmquist bias)
- **Population evolution**: SN progenitors at high-z may have different metallicity/age
- **K-correction failures**: rest-frame wavelength shifts at high-z stress the color model
- **SALT2 model breakdown**: the standardization model may simply perform worse at high-z

The critical question was: **does the signal correlate with the baryonic channel?** Tests 1-3 say **no**. If Closure drift operates through the baryonic medium (free electrons, plasma), we should see the effect scale with how much baryonic matter the light traversed. We don't.

### Possible explanations for the disconnect:
1. **Planck y-map may not be the right tracer** — tSZ traces hot gas in clusters, not the diffuse IGM where most baryons live. A better tracer might be FRB DM (which tracks ALL free electrons, not just cluster gas).
2. **The effect may be too subtle for Planck resolution** — Planck beam is ~10', smearing out structure below that scale.
3. **The accumulation signal in Test 4 is real but not caused by channel physics** — it's population/selection evolution, and the channel hypothesis is wrong.

## Next Steps (if continuing)

1. **Control for known systematics**: Repeat Test 4 but control for host galaxy mass, survey, and rest-frame wavelength coverage. If the accumulation disappears after controlling for these, it's population evolution.
2. **Try different baryonic tracers**: FRB DM field, X-ray background, HI column density maps instead of tSZ y.
3. **Test with different SN samples**: Use DES-5YR or Union3 catalogs independently.
4. **Spectral analysis**: If you can get SN spectra (not just photometry), look for line-width or fine-structure degradation as a function of z.
