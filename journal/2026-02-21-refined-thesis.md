# 2026-02-21 — Refined Thesis Statement

## Humza's Formulation (post-Round 3)

> A cosmic closure boundary should not primarily dim light; it should primarily collapse degrees of freedom in what light can tell you — producing a nonlinear (often sigmoid-like) loss of separability and a concentration of variance into fewer latent modes as redshift increases.

## Why This Is Better Than The Original

The original framing: "photons undergo cumulative information-channel degradation."

The refined version is more specific in five ways:
1. **Not dimming** — the effect is on separability, not amplitude
2. **Not gradual** — nonlinear/sigmoid activation
3. **Not all observables** — degrees of freedom collapse (specific ones, frequency-dependent)
4. **Not random** — variance concentrates into fewer latent modes (systematic compression)
5. **Not caused by matter** — it's a boundary property, not an environmental one
