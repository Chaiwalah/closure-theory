\title{A Rotating Standardization Manifold in Type~Ia Supernovae\\and the Geometric Failure of Fixed-Basis Tripp Corrections}

\author{Humza~Hafeez}
\email{humza@closure-theory.org}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher; ORCID: 0009-0000-0853-8807}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We show that the dominant principal axis of the $(x_1, c, \Delta\mu)$ standardization space of Type~Ia supernovae rotates by $\sim\!20^\circ$ across the Pantheon+ redshift range and progressively exits the fixed Tripp $(x_1, c)$ plane. At $z > 0.5$, only 50\% of the leading variance component projects onto the Tripp basis, compared to 76\% at $z < 0.06$. The Gram matrix of the $(x_1, c)$ subspace shows growing non-orthogonality, with a geometric excess reaching $-28\%$ at high redshift. Constant-coefficient Tripp standardization applied to this rotating manifold necessarily produces redshift-structured residuals: the observed Spearman correlation $\rho(z, \Delta\mu) = -0.199$ ($8.2\sigma$ above the null from 1{,}000 $\Lambda$CDM mocks) is a geometric inevitability, not a cosmological signal. A first-order repair---linear redshift-dependent coefficients $\alpha(z)$, $\beta(z)$, $\gamma(z)$ and a quadratic color term $\delta c^2$---partially tracks the rotation and reduces the anomaly by 32\% ($\Delta\chi^2 = 601$). Under this modified standardization, the $w_0 w_a$ dark energy parameterization collapses from $w_a = -1.88$ to $w_a = -0.02$, and $\Lambda$CDM plus modified Tripp outperforms $w_0 w_a$ plus standard Tripp by $\Delta\chi^2 = 37$. We further show that 71\% of the SN~Ia host-galaxy mass step originates in the color channel. Analogous coupling degradation observed independently in 750{,}414 SDSS~DR16Q quasars and 535 CHIME fast radio bursts rules out a pipeline-specific origin. These results imply that the dominant evidence for time-varying dark energy from SN~Ia samples is a projection artifact of applying a fixed coordinate system to a rotating standardization manifold.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%% ====================================================================

The Tripp standardization \citep{Tripp1998}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tripp}
\mu = m_B + \alpha\, x_1 - \beta\, c - \gamma\,\delta_M - M_B
\end{equation}
has been the foundation of SN~Ia cosmology since the discovery of cosmic acceleration \citep{Riess1998,Perlmutter1999}. It assumes that the light-curve stretch $x_1$, color $c$, and host-galaxy mass step $\delta_M$ span a \emph{fixed} coordinate system whose projection onto the distance modulus is redshift-independent.

We demonstrate that this assumption is geometrically false. The principal axes of the $(x_1, c, \Delta\mu)$ space rotate with redshift, progressively exiting the Tripp plane. At $z > 0.5$, half the dominant variance component lies outside the plane that Tripp models. A fixed projection of a rotating signal \emph{must} produce structured residuals. The $8.2\sigma$ residual--redshift correlation in Pantheon+ is not a hint of new physics---it is a geometric inevitability.

Throughout this work we adopt a flat $\Lambda$CDM reference cosmology with $H_0 = 70\;\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$ and $\Omega_m = 0.3$.

\section{Data}
\label{sec:data}
%% ====================================================================

We use the Pantheon+ compilation \citep{Scolnic2022}, applying quality cuts $0.01 < z_{\rm HD} < 2.5$, $|x_1| < 5$, $|c| < 0.5$, $\sigma_{m_B} < 1.0$, and $0 < \log(M_*/M_\odot) < 15$, retaining $N = 1{,}590$ SNe. Under standard Tripp (Eq.~\ref{eq:tripp}), the best-fit parameters are $\alpha = 0.139$, $\beta = 2.89$, $\gamma = -0.046$, $M_B = -19.315$, with $\chi^2/\nu = 13.6$ (consistent with covariance overestimation; \citealt{Keeley2023}). The Spearman residual--redshift correlation is $\rho = -0.199$ ($p = 1.1 \times 10^{-15}$).

To establish that this correlation is anomalous, we generate 1{,}000 mock catalogs by drawing $m_B$ from the best-fit $\Lambda$CDM model with Gaussian noise matching $\sigma_{m_B}$, keeping $(x_1, c, M_*)$ fixed. The null distribution has $\rho_{\rm null} = 0.000 \pm 0.024$. The observed $\rho = -0.199$ lies at $8.2\sigma$.

\subsection{Gram Matrix Non-Orthogonality}
\label{sec:gram}

The Tripp equation implicitly assumes that $x_1$ and $c$ span an approximately orthogonal basis. We test this by computing the Gram matrix $G_{ij} = \langle \hat{x}_i \hat{x}_j \rangle$ for the normalized $(x_1, c)$ subspace in each redshift bin. The determinant of $G$ measures the effective volume of the basis: $\det G = 1$ for an orthonormal system.

The geometric excess grows from $-8.5\%$ at low $z$ to $-28.3\%$ at high $z$: the Tripp basis becomes increasingly non-orthogonal. Fixed coefficients applied to a non-orthogonal, rotating basis necessarily leave structured residuals.

\subsection{Geometric Inevitability}
\label{sec:inevitability}

The combination of PC1 drainage, basis rotation, and growing non-orthogonality constitutes a geometric proof that constant Tripp standardization \emph{must} fail at high redshift. No physical mechanism is required: the failure follows from the mathematical properties of a fixed 2D projection applied to a signal whose dominant subspace rotates and dilates with redshift. The $8.2\sigma$ residual--redshift correlation is not a cosmological anomaly---it is a projection artifact.

\section{First-Order Repair}
\label{sec:repair}
%% ====================================================================

\subsection{Evolving Coefficients}
\label{sec:evolving}

As a first-order approximation to tracking the manifold rotation, we replace the constant Tripp coefficients with linear functions of redshift and add a quadratic color term:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:modified}
\mu = m_B + \alpha(z)\,x_1 - \beta(z)\,c - \delta\, c^2 - \gamma(z)\,\delta_M - M_B \,,
\end{equation}
with $\alpha(z) = \alpha_0 + \alpha_1 z$, $\beta(z) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 z$, and $\gamma(z) = \gamma_0 + \gamma_1 z$.

Best-fit values:
\begin{align}
\alpha(z) &= 0.148 - 0.032\,z \,, \\
\beta(z)  &= 3.11 - 0.62\,z \,, \\
\gamma(z) &= -0.028 - 0.070\,z \,, \\
\delta     &= +3.64 \,.
\end{align}

This improves over standard Tripp by $\Delta\chi^2 = 601$ for five additional parameters. The residual--redshift correlation reduces to $\rho = -0.135$ (32\% reduction), confirmed at $4.6\sigma$ by mock null injection.

The coefficient trends are consistent with the measured rotation: $\alpha$ decreases by 42\% (stretch loses standardization power as PC1 rotates away from $x_1$), $\beta$ decreases by 35\% (color--luminosity relation flattens as the color axis loses alignment), and $|\gamma|$ grows by a factor of 3.5 (host-galaxy correlations, partially degenerate with color, strengthen as the color channel fails).

\subsection{Quadratic Color and Manifold Curvature}
\label{sec:c2}

The quadratic color term $\delta c^2 = +3.64$ ($\Delta\chi^2 = 339$) indicates that the linear Tripp color correction is insufficient: the color--luminosity relation is curved, consistent with color encoding at least two latent variables (intrinsic SED variation and line-of-sight reddening). This curvature evolves: $\delta(z) = 3.51 + 1.97z$ ($p = 0.0014$), reflecting the increasing non-linearity of the manifold at high redshift.

\subsection{Mass Step Decomposition}
\label{sec:mass}

The standard Tripp mass step is $\gamma = -0.046$~mag. A stretch-only fit yields $\gamma = -0.014$~mag: \textbf{71\% of the mass step is color-channel driven}. This resolves a longstanding puzzle \citep{Kelly2010,Sullivan2010,Childress2014}: the mass step is predominantly an artifact of applying a constant color coefficient to a population where color--luminosity coupling varies with host properties.

\label{sec:w0wa}

Under the $w_0 w_a$ parameterization \citep{Chevallier2001,Linder2003}:

99\% of the $w_a$ signal is absorbed by the modified standardization. $\Lambda$CDM with modified Tripp ($\chi^2 = 9{,}775$) outperforms $w_0 w_a$ with standard Tripp ($\chi^2 = 9{,}811$) by $\Delta\chi^2 = 37$.

This is a direct consequence of the geometric failure: the $w_0 w_a$ parameterization, as a smooth function of redshift, is mathematically degenerate with redshift-dependent standardization corrections. When the manifold rotation is accounted for in the standardization, the cosmological free parameters have no residual structure left to absorb.

\subsection{Stretch-Only $H_0$}
\label{sec:h0}

Removing the color correction entirely shifts $H_0$ from $73.0$ to $70.5\;\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$. Incorporating $\alpha(z)$ evolution further reduces the value to $H_0 \approx 68.8$, within $1.4\;\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$ of the Planck CMB value. The color contribution to $H_0$ grows monotonically with redshift ($\rho = -0.800$), consistent with the progressive drainage of the color axis from the Tripp plane.

\section{Cross-Domain Evidence}
\label{sec:crossdomain}
%% ====================================================================

A pipeline-specific systematic origin for the manifold rotation is ruled out by analogous coupling degradation in two independent source classes.

Using 750{,}414 quasars from SDSS DR16Q \citep{Lyke2020}, the equivalent-width correlation between diagnostic emission-line pairs (e.g., C\,\textsc{iv}--Mg\,\textsc{ii}) collapses from $r = 0.82$ at $z \sim 0.5$ to $r = 0.03$ at $z \sim 2$, following a sigmoid threshold at $z_0 \approx 1.1$. Kinematic observables (FWHM ratios) are immune.

Using 535 FRBs from CHIME/FRB Catalog~1 \citep{CHIME2021} plus 186 localized events, the temporal width--spectral index correlation vanishes beyond DM $\approx 500\;\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$. The three source classes span five orders of magnitude in frequency and entirely independent pipelines, yet exhibit the same pattern: frequency-dependent coupling degradation with path length, sigmoid thresholds, and kinematic immunity.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}
%% ====================================================================

\begin{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Residual floor.} The modified Tripp residual correlation ($\rho = -0.135$) remains significant ($7.0\sigma$ above the diagonal null). The linear repair tracks only the first-order rotation; the residual likely reflects manifold curvature and saturation at $z > 1$, where the $[1.0, 1.5)$ bin shows over-correction.

\item \textbf{Covariance mismatch.} The public Pantheon+ covariance is defined in post-standardization $\mu$-space. Our corrections operate in the upstream $(m_B, x_1, c)$ space. Full-covariance analysis requires either the SALT2 parameter-level covariance or integration within the SNANA pipeline.

\item \textbf{DES comparison.} The DES-SN5YR BBC-corrected residuals show $\rho = -0.018$, indicating that sophisticated bias-correction pipelines can absorb the same class of basis non-stationarity. This is consistent with our interpretation: BBC implicitly allows the standardization basis to flex without naming the geometric mechanism. Raw DES $(x_1, c)$ values are not publicly available for direct comparison.

\item \textbf{PCA bin dependence.} The exact PC1 projection fractions depend on bin boundaries and sample composition. The qualitative trend---progressive drainage from the Tripp plane---is robust across alternative binning schemes.

\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}

\item \textbf{Gravitational-wave standard sirens.} GW distance measurements involve no electromagnetic standardization basis. We predict zero coefficient evolution ($\alpha(z) = \beta(z) = \gamma(z) = \mathrm{const}$) and no manifold rotation in GW-inferred Hubble diagrams.

\item \textbf{DESI stretch-only reanalysis.} Stretch-only standardization of the DESI SN sample should yield $H_0 \approx 68.8\;\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$ and substantially reduce the reported $w_a$ deviation.

\item \textbf{Euclid eigenvector rotation.} The Euclid SN sample should show directly measurable PC1 rotation exceeding $15^\circ$ between $z = 0.5$ and $z = 1.5$.

\item \textbf{TDCOSMO lenses.} Re-calibrating TDCOSMO time-delay distances with stretch-only SN anchors should shift $H_0$ by $\sim 2\;\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}}$ toward the Planck value.

\end{enumerate}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%% ====================================================================

The central result of this work is geometric, not cosmological. We have shown that the principal axes of the SN~Ia standardization space rotate with redshift and that the Tripp equation---a fixed 2D projection---progressively loses contact with the dominant variance component. This is not a model; it is a measurement. The rotation angle, the drainage fraction, and the growing non-orthogonality are properties of the Pantheon+ data, independent of any physical interpretation.

The physical origin of the rotation remains an open question. One candidate is a propagation effect in which the expanding metric preferentially degrades correlations between observables in proportion to the number of independent coupling channels through which information can be lost. Under this framework, color (sensitive to dust, metallicity, SED shape, and reddening law) degrades faster than stretch (primarily sensitive to $^{56}$Ni mass), producing exactly the observed hierarchy. The cross-domain recurrence in quasars and FRBs supports a propagation-level origin, but a definitive theoretical derivation is beyond the scope of this work.

The practical implication is immediate: dark energy constraints from SN~Ia samples carry a geometric systematic that current constant-coefficient analyses do not model. The DESI collaboration's report of $w_a = -0.75 \pm 0.29$ \citep{DESI2024} should be revisited under basis-aware standardization.

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}
%% ====================================================================

\begin{enumerate}

\item The dominant principal axis of the $(x_1, c, \Delta\mu)$ standardization space rotates $\sim\!20^\circ$ across the Pantheon+ redshift range. At $z > 0.5$, 50\% of the leading variance exits the Tripp plane. Constant-coefficient standardization of a rotating manifold necessarily produces structured residuals.

\item The observed residual--redshift correlation ($\rho = -0.199$, $8.2\sigma$) is a geometric projection artifact, not a cosmological signal.

\item A first-order repair (evolving $\alpha(z)$, $\beta(z)$, $\gamma(z)$, plus $c^2$) reduces the anomaly by 32\% ($\Delta\chi^2 = 601$) and absorbs 99\% of the $w_a$ dark energy signal ($w_a: -1.88 \to -0.02$).

\item $\Lambda$CDM with modified Tripp outperforms $w_0 w_a$ with standard Tripp by $\Delta\chi^2 = 37$.

\item 71\% of the host-galaxy mass step is color-channel driven.

\item Analogous degradation in quasars and FRBs rules out a pipeline-specific origin.

\end{enumerate}

The simplest interpretation: the crisis is in the coordinate system, not the cosmology.
