## Abstract

We propose Closure Theory, a unifying framework describing how stable, classical, and autonomous behavior emerges across domains when internal correlation density exceeds the effective information bandwidth available across a system boundary. Rather than introducing new dynamics or modifying existing physical laws, Closure Theory reinterprets the domains of validity of descriptions as phase regimes of information flow.

We formalize this principle using a dimensionless closure ratio:

**C = I_internal / B_accessible**

where I_internal denotes internal mutual information or correlation density, and B_accessible denotes the effective bandwidth through which information can be resolved, transmitted, or acted upon across the system boundary.

We identify three universal phases:
1. **Residual Structure** (C << 1)
2. **Active Closure** (C ~ 1)  
3. **Inaccessible Continuation** (C > 1)

We demonstrate that this structure reproduces known results in quantum decoherence, thermodynamic irreversibility, biological individuation, neural learning (including grokking), artificial intelligence phase transitions, and cosmological structure formation -- without invoking new forces, dimensions, or ontological commitments.

Closure Theory provides a substrate-independent criterion for when micro-level descriptions cease to be operationally relevant, explaining the emergence of classicality, objectivity, and irreversibility as consequences of finite bandwidth rather than fundamental distinctions.

## 1. Motivation and Scope

Modern science operates with extraordinary success across domains, yet repeatedly encounters conceptual discontinuities:

- quantum vs. classical
- micro vs. macro
- reversible vs. irreversible
- local vs. global
- descriptive vs. actionable models

These discontinuities are typically treated as ontological divides or scale-dependent laws. Closure Theory advances a different thesis:

**Many apparent foundational divides arise from bandwidth-limited information flow across system boundaries, not from changes in underlying dynamics.**

This framework does not replace existing theories. It does not claim privileged access to global truth. It instead provides a diagnostic criterion for when particular descriptions become sufficient, dominant, or obsolete for prediction and control.

## 2. Core Principle: Finite Bandwidth and Information Trapping

All real systems interact with their environment through finite channels. No physical, biological, or cognitive boundary permits infinite resolution or transmission of correlations. As internal interactions generate correlations, redundancy accumulates. When this accumulation exceeds the boundary's representational capacity, information becomes effectively trapped.

At that point:
- micro-level distinctions cease to propagate outward
- coarse-grained variables dominate
- autonomous, classical-like behavior emerges

This transition is structural, not dynamical.

## 3. The Closure Ratio

**C = I_internal / B_accessible**

### Definitions

- **I_internal**: Total internal mutual information, redundancy, or correlation density generated by interactions within the system.
- **B_accessible**: Effective information bandwidth across the system boundary, including resolution, coherence, and transmissibility constraints.

### Closure Regimes

- **C << 1** -- Open, delocalized, quantum-like
- **C ~ 1** -- Transitional, emergent, mixed-description
- **C > 1** -- Closed, classical, autonomous

Importantly, C is observer-relative by construction, defined only with respect to an embedded agent or subsystem.

---

## 4. Three Universal Phases of Description

### Phase I -- Residual Structure (C << 1)

Correlations exist but do not saturate bandwidth. Fine-grained distinctions remain accessible, but no autonomous entity has yet stabilized.

Examples:
- Quantum superpositions prior to decoherence
- Cosmic microwave background anisotropies
- Genetic remnants and fossil records
- Distant starlight before structural closure

These structures are observable but not agentive.

### Phase II -- Active Closure (C ~ 1)

Internal correlations approach boundary bandwidth. Competing descriptions coexist. The system becomes highly sensitive to perturbation and reorganizes into stable collective variables.

Examples:
- Quantum measurement and pointer states
- Star and galaxy formation
- Protein folding
- Neural integration
- AI grokking transitions

Observers exist only in this phase.

### Phase III -- Inaccessible Continuation (C > 1)

Correlation production overwhelms bandwidth. Further internal dynamics become structurally inaccessible, appearing as horizons or irreversibility.

Examples:
- Black hole interiors
- Cosmological horizons
- Subjective conscious experience boundaries
- Death and irreversible biological transitions

This phase reflects informational inaccessibility, not metaphysical absence.

## 8. Five Foundational Laws

### Law 1: Closure Law
When C = I/B >= 1, the system undergoes a phase transition into a new effective classical layer.

### Law 2: Constraint-as-Policy Law
The closure boundary acts as a selective information filter (policy), not as a force. It does not push, scatter, or redirect -- it selectively determines which correlations propagate and which are trapped.

### Law 3: Local Access Law
All measurements and descriptions are observer-relative, defined only with respect to an embedded agent's bandwidth. There is no global view.

### Law 4: UV-IR Inaccessibility Duality
Information becomes inaccessible at both extremes of scale -- very small (UV) and very large (IR) -- for the same structural reason: the observer's bandwidth is finite in both directions.

### Law 5: Emergent Time Law
Time is not the independent variable of evolution. The true evolution parameter is cumulative information production: integral I(t)dt. Time is a coordinate on the information manifold.

## 9. Cosmological Predictions (Made Before Empirical Testing)

### Prediction 1: Selective Spectral Degradation
Spectral correlations between astronomical observables should degrade selectively with distance, with degradation rates proportional to diagnostic sensitivity. Observables locked to fundamental constants should be immune.

### Prediction 2: Sigmoid Thresholds
The transition from intact to degraded correlations should follow a sigmoid function: Gamma(C) = Gamma0 sigma((C - C*)/Delta), producing sharp thresholds rather than gradual decline.

### Prediction 3: Channel Specificity
The degradation should be channel-dependent (affecting some observable properties but not others), not geometric (affecting everything equally).

### Prediction 4: Bandwidth Recovery
Increasing effective bandwidth (e.g., through denser matter along the sightline, higher SNR, or better spectral resolution) should partially restore correlations that are degraded at lower bandwidth.

### Prediction 5: Cross-Domain Consistency
The same pattern should appear across independent source classes (different wavelengths, different physics) because the principle is substrate-independent.

### Prediction 6: Hubble Tension
If spectral and geometric measurement channels have different effective bandwidths, systematic disagreement between methods is a natural consequence, not an anomaly.

### Prediction 7: JWST "Impossible" Galaxies
Spectral age/mass inference at high redshift may be systematically biased by diagnostic compression, making galaxies appear more mature than their actual formation epoch.

## 11. Falsifiability Criteria

Closure Theory is falsified if any of the following occur:

1. Classical objectivity arises in systems with persistently low redundancy.
2. Closed macroscopic laws remain sensitive to micro-level resolution.
3. Systems with identical closure ratios show inconsistent emergent behavior.
4. Increasing bandwidth restores full micro-control in closed regimes.
5. Major evolutionary transitions occur without boundary formation.
6. Neural grokking fails to correlate with internal mutual information crossing a threshold.

Any one of these invalidates the framework.
